# Indian Election Process — Official Knowledge

## Overview
India holds elections for Parliament (Lok Sabha), State Assemblies (Vidhan Sabha), and local bodies. Elections are conducted by the Election Commission of India (ECI), an independent constitutional body.

## 12 Steps of the Electoral Lifecycle

### Step 1: Voter Eligibility
- Must be an Indian citizen
- Must be at least 18 years old on the qualifying date (January 1 of the reference year)
- Must be ordinarily resident in the polling area
- Must not be of unsound mind or disqualified under any law

### Step 2: Voter Registration
- Fill Form 6 online at voters.eci.gov.in or Voter Helpline App
- Required documents: passport photo, address proof, age proof
- You can also submit at the Electoral Registration Officer's office
- Registration is free

### Step 3: Voter List / Electoral Roll
- Your name must appear in the official Electoral Roll for your constituency
- Having a Voter ID card (EPIC) alone is not enough
- Check at electoralsearch.eci.gov.in
- Electoral Roll is updated periodically; check before each election

### Step 4: Election Announcement
- The President of India issues a notification on the advice of the Council of Ministers
- ECI announces the election schedule including dates for nomination, voting, and counting
- This triggers the Model Code of Conduct

### Step 5: Model Code of Conduct (MCC)
- A set of guidelines issued by ECI
- Applies to all political parties and candidates from announcement to result declaration
- Restricts use of government machinery, hate speech, and voter inducement

### Step 6: Candidate Nomination
- Any eligible citizen can file a nomination
- Requirements include a nomination form, security deposit, and valid voter ID
- Nominations are scrutinized by the Returning Officer
- Candidates can withdraw within a specified period

### Step 7: Campaigning
- Parties and candidates campaign within MCC rules
- Campaigning must stop 48 hours before polling day (silent period)
- Paid news, hate speech, and bribery of voters are prohibited

### Step 8: Polling Day
- Voters go to their designated polling station
- Identity verified using EPIC or approved alternative IDs (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport, etc.)
- Indelible ink applied to left index finger
- Voter presses button on EVM

### Step 9: EVM / VVPAT Voting
- Electronic Voting Machine (EVM): Voting unit and Control Unit
- VVPAT (Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail): Prints a slip showing your vote for 7 seconds
- The VVPAT slip is not a receipt — it falls into a sealed box
- EVMs are standalone devices; they are not connected to internet

### Step 10: Vote Counting
- Counting begins on the date announced by ECI
- Done under strict surveillance at Counting Centres
- Counting agents of candidates are present
- Postal ballots counted first

### Step 11: Result Declaration
- The candidate with the highest votes in a constituency wins (First Past The Post system)
- The Returning Officer declares the result
- Results are uploaded on results.eci.gov.in

### Step 12: Government Formation
- After Lok Sabha elections, the President invites the party/coalition with majority (272+ seats) to form government
- If no single party has majority, coalition negotiations happen
- The Prime Minister is appointed and then Cabinet is formed
