# Politics Basics — Indian Democracy

## What is Democracy?
Democracy is a system of government where the people of a country choose their representatives through free and fair elections. India is a parliamentary democracy and a federal republic.

## The Constitution of India
- Came into effect on January 26, 1950 (Republic Day)
- Declares India a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic
- Contains Fundamental Rights, Directive Principles, and Fundamental Duties
- Can be amended by Parliament through a special procedure

## Parliament of India
- India has a bicameral Parliament (two houses)
- **Lok Sabha** (House of the People): Lower house, 543 elected seats, Members called MPs
- **Rajya Sabha** (Council of States): Upper house, 245 seats, Members elected by State Assemblies

## State Government
- Each state has a **Vidhan Sabha** (Legislative Assembly) — the lower house
- Some states also have a **Vidhan Parishad** (Legislative Council)
- State governments are headed by a **Chief Minister**

## Election Commission of India (ECI)
- Independent constitutional body established in 1950
- Responsible for supervising, directing, and controlling all elections in India
- Led by the Chief Election Commissioner (CEC)
- Publishes Electoral Rolls, manages EVMs, and enforces the Model Code of Conduct

## Political Parties in India
- India has a multi-party system
- Parties are classified as National Parties or State Parties by ECI
- To be a National Party, a party must meet specific vote-share and seat criteria across states
- As of the latest ECI recognition, recognized national parties include BJP, INC, BSP, CPI(M), NPP, and AAP

## What is a Manifesto?
- A formal public statement of a party's goals, policies, and promises if elected
- Parties release manifestos before elections
- Voters can read them to understand what a party stands for
- Manifestos are not legally binding

## What is a Coalition Government?
- When no single party wins a majority on its own, multiple parties come together to form a government
- The combined group is called a coalition
- The largest party in the coalition typically provides the Prime Minister

## What is the Opposition?
- Parties that do not form the government sit in opposition in Parliament
- The largest opposition party leads the "Official Opposition"
- The opposition's role is to scrutinize the government, debate legislation, and present alternatives

## What is NOTA?
- NOTA stands for "None of the Above"
- It was introduced in India from the 2013 state elections onwards
- It allows voters to officially reject all candidates on the ballot
- NOTA votes are counted but a candidate can still win even if NOTA gets more votes than them in the current system

## What is a By-Election?
- Also called a by-poll
- Held for a single constituency when a seat becomes vacant (due to death, resignation, disqualification)
- Does not change the overall composition significantly
