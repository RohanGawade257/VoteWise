# First-Time Voter Guide — India

## Introduction
Voting for the first time is an important civic milestone. This guide helps you through every step.

## Step 1: Check Your Eligibility
- Must be an Indian citizen
- Must be 18 years or older on the qualifying date (usually January 1 of the election year)
- Must be a resident of the constituency where you want to register

## Step 2: Register as a Voter
- Go to voters.eci.gov.in or download the Voter Helpline App
- Fill out Form 6
- Upload a passport-size photo, proof of age (birth certificate or 10th mark sheet), and proof of address (Aadhaar, electricity bill, or rent agreement)
- You can also visit your local Electoral Registration Officer (ERO) office

## Step 3: Check Your Name in the Voter List
- After registration, always verify your name is in the Electoral Roll
- Visit electoralsearch.eci.gov.in and search by your name, EPIC number, or mobile
- Your name in the roll is what matters most — not just having a Voter ID card

## Step 4: Find Your Polling Booth
- Once registered, you are assigned a specific polling station (booth)
- Find your booth at voters.eci.gov.in or via the 1950 Voter Helpline
- Your booth address is on your Voter Information Slip (VIS), which booths agents distribute before elections

## Step 5: Know What ID to Carry
- Primary: Voter ID card (EPIC)
- Alternatives accepted by ECI: Aadhaar card, PAN card, Passport, Driving License, MNREGA Job Card, Smart Card issued by RGI under NPR, Pension document with photo, Central/State Government employee identity card
- Always carry the physical, original card — photocopies and phone photos are not accepted

## Step 6: Vote on Polling Day
1. Go to your designated polling station on polling day
2. Check in with the Presiding Officer — your name will be found in the voter list
3. Your finger will be marked with indelible ink on the left index finger
4. Enter the voting compartment
5. Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate's name and symbol on the EVM
6. The VVPAT will show a printed slip of your vote for 7 seconds through a glass window
7. Leave the polling station

## Step 7: Understand What Happens Next
- Votes are counted on the date announced by ECI
- The candidate with the most votes in your constituency wins
- Results are announced on results.eci.gov.in
- Even if your candidate does not win, the winning candidate represents your entire constituency

## Important Notes
- Voting is a right, not a compulsion in India (unlike some countries)
- Your vote is completely secret — no one can find out who you voted for
- Voter ID cards are also used as proof of identity for many other purposes
